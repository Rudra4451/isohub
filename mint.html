How to Install Linux Mint

  • 1
    Download the Cinnamon ISO from the official Linux Mint site above. About 2.8 GB.
  • 2
    Use Rufus (Windows) or balenaEtcher to flash the ISO to your USB drive (8 GB+).
  • 3
    Back up your data then restart your PC and press F12/F2/DEL to open the boot menu.
  • 4
    Select the USB drive. Linux Mint boots into a live desktop — you can try it before installing.
  • 5
    Double-click "Install Linux Mint" on the desktop and follow the wizard. Choose "Erase disk" for a clean install.
  • 6
    Set up your username and password. Installation finishes in 10–20 minutes. Remove USB and restart.
💡 Tip: The live session lets you browse the internet and test software before installing anything. Your USB becomes a portable OS you can run on any computer!
